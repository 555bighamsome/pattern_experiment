Pattern Programming Experiment

Welcome!

In this experiment, you'll be solving visual puzzles by creating simple programs. Think of yourself as a designer who needs to recreate target patterns using basic building blocks and operations.

You'll see 18 target patterns displayed on a 10×10 grid. Your job is to recreate each pattern exactly by composing a program using:

  • Primitive shapes (lines, squares, triangles, etc.)
  • Operations (add, subtract, union, flip, etc.)

When your program creates a pattern that exactly matches the target, you'll earn bonus points!

Creating Reusable Building Blocks

Here's where it gets interesting: as you work, you might notice that certain patterns or sub-patterns could be useful later.

You can save any program you create to your personal collection:

  • Saved programs appear as visual thumbnails you can see and select
  • These saved programs carry forward to all future tasks
  • You can use them as building blocks in more complex patterns
  • Think of them as creating your own custom "super-shapes"

Strategy tip: The patterns get progressively more complex. Programs you save early on might become useful shortcuts later!

We're interested in understanding how people break down complex visual problems and build reusable solutions. There are no right or wrong approaches—work in whatever way feels natural to you.

  • There's no time limit—work at your own pace
  • Program length doesn't matter—use as many or as few steps as you need
  • You'll see your progress (Pattern x of 18) throughout
